Highlight text to reveal rhetorical techniques and get a neutral rewrite. Color-coded annotations, powered by Claude AI.

## Detailed description

Neutralize analyzes the language of anything you read online — news articles, opinion pieces, political speeches, social media posts — and tells you exactly what rhetorical moves are being made and why.

**How it works**

Highlight 3–5 sentences on any webpage. Neutralize's side panel opens with your text color-coded by rhetorical technique:

- 🟠 **Rhetorical appeals** — pathos, logos, ethos
- 🔵 **Structural devices** — repetition, alliteration, comparison
- 🟢 **Meaning & connotation** — loaded language, word choice, framing

Click any highlighted phrase to see a tooltip explaining its rhetorical role and suggesting neutral alternatives with different connotations. Scroll down for a complete neutral rewrite of the passage and an explanation of what the author was likely trying to achieve.

**Why Neutralize?**

Most of us consume news, social media, and opinion writing without noticing how language shapes our reactions. Rhetorical techniques aren't inherently manipulative — but being unaware of them makes you more susceptible to them. Neutralize makes the invisible visible.

**Privacy**

Neutralize does not collect any personal data. Your API key is stored locally on your device using your browser's built-in storage and is never sent anywhere except Anthropic's API endpoint. No analytics, no tracking, no accounts required.

**Requirements**

Neutralize requires an Anthropic API key (free to obtain at console.anthropic.com). Analysis calls use the Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 model and are billed directly to your Anthropic account.

**Open source**

Source code is available at github.com/wleejess/neutralize.
